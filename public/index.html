How to Play Pure Bluff

Complete worldwide rules for the Bluff (also known as Cheat) card game.

🎯 Objective

Be the last player holding cards, or force others to collect the entire deck. Players strategically bid on card ranks while being able to bluff — claim cards they may not actually have.

🃏 Setup

  • 2–6 players: use 1 standard deck (52 cards)
  • 7–10 players: use 2 standard decks (104 cards)
  • Shuffle and deal all cards equally
  • Some players may have one extra card

🔄 Turn Structure

  1. Player announces: Quantity × Rank (e.g. "3 Aces")
  2. Each bid must exceed previous (higher rank OR more quantity)
  3. Next player: bid higher OR challenge "BLUFF!"
  4. First bid of a round cannot be challenged
  5. You may always increase quantity at same rank

⚡ Challenge (Bluff!)

  • Any player can call BLUFF on the current bid
  • All cards in the central pile are revealed
  • If bid is TRUE: challenger picks up all pile cards
  • If bid is FALSE: bidder picks up all pile cards
  • New round starts with the loser going first

🏆 Winning

The winner is the last player remaining with cards. Players are eliminated when they successfully empty their hand — but they must be called on a valid bid, or bluff successfully when called. The game continues until one player holds all the cards (they lose), or only one player has cards left (they win).

📋 Card Ranks

A K Q J 10 9 8 7 6 5 4 3 2

Ace is highest, 2 is lowest. Suits are irrelevant for ranking.
